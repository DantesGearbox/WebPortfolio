Aim for the Top

This prototype was made for a university course in one weeks by me and a team using Unity. The assignment was to choose one or several feelings and make a game that conveyed these to the player. The feelings we settled for were amused confusion and frustration. To convey these feelings we thought of making a game where you did something very simple, but it would be unnecesarily hard. I worked on the team as a gameplay-designer and programmer.

Game and Rules

Aim for the top is a competitive game where you have to get as high up as possible by jumping on platforms, but the first twist is: every time you jump your jump-button gets randomly re-mapped to a different button on the controller. The second twist is: there is only one controller, and you and your opponent has to share. Well. "Share" might not be the best word to describe it. Dirty tricks and "physical pursuation" is allowed.

Development Process

This game was ended up being very experimental, random and occasionally fun. To try and make the game less random we tried many different versions of this game. One version there were three different controllers that your jump-button might be bound to, and still only two players. This idea sounded like fun, but was too random. Players ended up pressing each others jump buttons as much as their own. In another version, to try and increase the frustration of the game, you'd start falling down through platforms if you hadn't found your jump button with-in some time frame. However, this cause too much frustration.

Requirements to play

You need to plug in an xbox360 controller (not PS4) and you need some kind of opponent. A human would be preferrable, but given the randomness of this game, a pet of some kind might also do fine.
